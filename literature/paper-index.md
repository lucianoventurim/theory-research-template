### Deb, Sugaya, and Wolitzky — random matching / folk theorem

Citation key: `Deb2020_FolkTheoremRepeatedGamesAnonymousRandomMatching`

PDF: `literature/pdfs/Deb, J., Sugaya, T., and Wolitzky, A. 2020. The Folk Theorem in Repeated Games With Anonymous Random Matching. ECTA.pdf`

Extracted text: `literature/extracted-text/Deb, J., Sugaya, T., and Wolitzky, A. 2020. The Folk Theorem in Repeated Games With Anonymous Random Matching. ECTA.txt`

Status: central

Use in project: Closely related to repeated interaction in large anonymous random-matching environments. Useful for framing equilibrium behavior and the role of large-population limits.

Difference from my project: Their main object is a folk-theorem-style equilibrium characterization, whereas my project studies coordinated learning and the informational conditions under which coordination emerges or fails.

### Wiseman — unknown payoffs

Citation key: `Wiseman2005_PartialFolkTheoremGamesUnknownPayoffDistributions`

PDF: `literature/pdfs/Wiseman, T. 2005. A Partial Folk Theorem for Games with Unknown Payoff Distributions. ECTA.pdf`

Extracted text: `literature/extracted-text/Wiseman, T. 2005. A Partial Folk Theorem for Games with Unknown Payoff Distributions. ECTA.txt`

Status: central

Use in project: Studies coordinated learning with a fixed set of players and public histories.

Difference from my project: Prove a folk-theorem-like result. It relies on punishments for deviating players, but it studies a more general payoff structure. In mine, we are interested in learning payoffs of a symmetric stage-games with Nash Equilibria, but in a random matching, continuum of players setting.
